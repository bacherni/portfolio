# Abstract

This study replicates and extends the Moral Contagion Theory by Brady et al. (2017), which argues that messages combining moral and emotional language are more likely to spread online. Using Reddit data instead of Twitter, the analysis examines whether moral-emotional expressions predict higher user engagement across four norm-oriented subreddits: r/AbortionDebate, r/CovidVaccinated, r/IsraelPalestine, and r/NeutralPolitics. Applying negative binomial regression models, results show that the impact of moral-emotional language varies substantially across communities. While it increases post visibility in some subreddits, it decreases it in others, suggesting that moral contagion is not a universal mechanism but strongly shaped by platform norms and context. These findings highlight the importance of platform-specific factors in understanding online discourse and the diffusion of moralized content.
